In the name of God amen I Hannah\
Kingham of Ilmer in the County of Bucks\
Widow being weak and infirm in body but of\
perfect and sound mind memory and\
understanding and finding my mortality\
Do hereby make ordain publish and declare this\
my last Will and Testament in writing as\
followeth revoking hereby and setting aside all\
former and other wills by me made either in

---

Writing or by word of mouth And first\
and principally I resign my soul into\
the hands of Almighty God who gave it me\
in hopes through the merits of my redeemer\
Jesus Christ to have pardon for all my sins\
and to have everlasting life and my body\
I commit to the earth to be decently interred\
at the discretion of my Executors herein after\
named and as to my temporal Estate\
which it hath pleased the almighty to\
enrich me with I give will and dispose of in\
manner and form as followeth And first I\
Give will and bequeath to my beloved son\
Joshua Kingham and my beloved daughter\
Sarah Kingham the sum of one Guinea apiece\
of lawful money of Great Britain to be paid\
to my said son Joshua and my daughter\
Sarah respectively by my Executors herein\
after named out of my personal estate in\
one year after my decease Also I Give will\
and bequeath unto my beloved daughters\
Rebecca Newatt and Ann Kingham the sum\
of fifty pounds apiece of the like lawful\
british money to be paid to my said two\
daughters Rebecca and Ann respectively out\
of my personal estate in one year after my\
decease by my Executors herein after named\
Also I Give will and bequeath unto my said

Daughter Rebecca Newatt my Yellow bedstead\
Curtains feather bed boulster and two pillows and all\
other the bedding and furniture belonging to the\
same and also my best oaken chest likewise my\
set of red and white China and one pair of sheets\
to go be and remain to my said daughter Rebecca\
Newatt her Executors and assigns to her and their\
own proper use behoof and disposal Also I Give\
will and bequeath unto my said daughter Ann\
Kingham my brown bedstead curtains feather\
bed boulster pillows and all other bedding and\
furniture belonging to the same Also I Give\
will and bequeath unto my said daughter Ann\
my other oaken Chest my set of blue and white\
China and one pair of sheets to go be and remain\
to my said daughter Ann her Executors and\
assigns to her and their own proper use behoof\
and disposal Also I Give will and bequeath\
unto my beloved son Thomas Kingham my\
feather bed bedstead boulster pillow and all other\
my bedding and furniture belonging to the\
same being the bed whereon he my said son\
Thomas usually sleeps upon to go be and remain to my said son\
Thomas his Executors and assigns to his and\
their own proper use ^behoof and disposal Also I Give\
will and bequeath unto my beloved son John\
Kingham of Long Wick in the parish of Princes\
Risborough in the said County of Bucks\
Husbandman and my said son Thomas

---

Thomas Kingham (after paying my just\
debts and funeral expences also the above\
mentioned legacies) All and singular and\
every my apparel money goods chattels\
credits stock of corn grain hay and all other\
my personal estate whatsoever herein\
before unbequeathed to go be and remain unto\
them to be equally divided between them share\
and share alike And I do hereby nominate\
ordain constitute and appoint my said two\
sons John and Thomas to be joint Executors\
to this my last Will and Testament contained\
in two sheets of paper unto the first of which\
I have set my hand and to the last my hand\
and seal this twenty fourth day of April\
in the year of our Lord one thousand seven\
hundred and seventy eight The mark of\
Hannah Kingham [Seal] Signed sealed published\
and declared by the testatrix as her last Will\
and Testament in the presence of us who\
have subscribed our names as witnesses to the\
same in the presence of the testatrix and at\
her request and also these presents being first\
interlined in three several places \
Thomas Williams + Joshua Kingham + Henry Lacey ./

---

Proved the 24th. day of April 1790. Before the\
Revd William Stockins Clerk Surrogate by the\
oath of Thomas Kingham the natural and\
lawful son and one of the Executors to whom\
